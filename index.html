Statement of Informed Consent

    You're about to participate in a research study. Your contribution to our research allows us to learn more about how opinions differ on Teach For America, or TFA.
    Please read the following information carefully before proceeding.

    Why we are doing this research

    We are trying to understand how people's opinions range and how they are spread out (eg. bell curve/normal spread) surrounding TFA. We are also trying to understand how commenting systems perform and affect engagement.

    What you will have to do

    You will be shown an article concerning Teach For America, taken from the Harvard Crimson. Feel free to make comments and reply to comments as you wish. There will be questions regarding basic information in the beginning and your opinion of the article afterwards.

    Potential risks

    There are no risks anticipated in taking part in this study and you are free to leave at any time.

    Duration

    Approximately 10 minutes.

    Whom to contact about your rights in this research, for questions, concerns, suggestions, or complaints that are not being addressed by the researcher, or research-related harm: Committee on the Use of Human Subjects in Research at Harvard University, 1414 Massachusetts Avenue, Second Floor, Cambridge, MA 02138. Instructions

    This test will investigate where you stand on the currently debated issue around TFA. Your task is to read the article and to interact with it as you see fit.
    A 10-minute timer will be put in place. If you finish reading after two minutes, you can go to the opinion section by clicking the 'Finish Reading' button.
    The opinion section will gauge where you stand on this issue.
    At the end, you will see how many others think the same way you do on this issue. Since the majority of our participant pool is coming from Harvard College, you will discover how divergent or convergent your opinion is to (other) Harvard students.
